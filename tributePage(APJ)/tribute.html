A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's Life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - Graduates in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955 - Enrolls at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to study Aerospace Engineering.
  • 1960 - Joins the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.
  • 1969 - Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). Becomes the Project Director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • 1981 - Receives the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors.
  • 1982 - Returns to DRDO and directs the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), leading to the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
  • 1990 - Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.
  • 1992 - Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1998 - Plays a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests, solidifying India's position as a nuclear state.
  • 1999 - Appointed as the Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. Known as the "People's President," he emphasizes youth involvement in nation-building and education.
  • 2007 - After his presidency, returns to teaching, writing, and public service. Becomes a visiting professor at various prestigious institutes across India.
  • 2010 - Launches the "What Can I Give Movement," aimed at defeating corruption through youth empowerment.
  • 2012 - Publishes "Turning Points: A Journey Through Challenges," an autobiographical account of his life after the presidency.
  • 2014 - Delivers the keynote address at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong, and continues to inspire students and young professionals across the nation.
  • 2015 - Passes away on July 27th while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His death is mourned nationwide, and he is remembered as the "Missile Man of India" and an inspirational leader.

"Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life and achievements are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence, and visionary leadership can make to national development and global peace."

-- Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
